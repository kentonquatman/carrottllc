About Carrott

Carrott helps companies build exceptional boards and managements.

Out clients trust us to help them find and select their leaders, even advising boards on CEO succession as at Rock-Tenn last year. That trust results from a history of objectivity, integrity and superb execution. As a result, we have deep relationships with our clients and works at multiple levels and in multiple ways. If an engagement is important to a client, it's important to us.

Consultants must measure their performance by the success of their clients, and the CEOs that we have helped public companies recruit have produced returns to shareholders that average 6.3 percent greater than the S&P 500 each year during their tenures.

In contrast, Forbes magazine reported companies that recruited a CEO using one of the four major search firms underperformed the S&P 500 by three percentage points over 10 years.

Client Benefits

Clients can make more disciplined decisions, and hence, better decisions about talent. Our team has some of the most respected advisors on talent anywhere with access to companies, boards and managements around the world, and it brings together decades of experience and a proprietary assessment methodology to solve client problems.

We offer clients facts, not opinions, and use data to frame decisions. The judgments we take on people or companies can be validated. It is the strength of the data we provide; it is the precision of our process that allows clients to make optimal decisions about talent. For example, our assessment protocol allows us to predict the fit of an individual with the culture of an organization and its style of decision making with statistical validity.

Biographies

Gregory T. Carrott

Chicago

Greg Carrott has worked in executive search for 25 years. His engagements include finding dozens of chief executive officers, including for Argonne National Laboratory, Brady Corporation, Cott Bottling, Federal Signal Corporation, Molson and Ontario Power Generation.

His years in search have included seven years at Heidrick & Struggles and 13 years at Egon Zehnder International. He came to search with significant management experience. He was a staff officer of Borg-Warner Corporation and a vice-president and management supervisor of a division of Young & Rubicam. Earlier, he worked for Chicago's Continental Bank and Northwest Industries Incorporated.

Carrott received a B.A. from Lawrence University, an M.S.J. from Northwestern University's Medill School of Journalism and an M.B.A. from the The University of Chicago. He served for five years on an advisory board of the strategy-consulting firm L.E.K. Consulting LLC and has written articles for The Corporate Board, Directorship, Directors & Boards and the Harvard Business Review.

Simon Harrison

London

Simon Harrison has more than 27 years of experience in executive search and management assessment.

Based in London, he joined Heidrick & Struggles in 1987 and led its U.K. insurance and retail financial services practice from 1990 with a broad international mandate, becoming a partner in 1995. He moved to Spencer Stuart in 2007 before founding Harrison Associates, an executive search boutique specializing in the insurance and wealth management sectors.

Though his executive search practice focuses principally on financial services, his experience in management assessment covers a variety of sectors, including manufacturing, consumer products, energy, utilities and media as well as insurance.

He graduated with his master's degree in law from Trinity College, Oxford, before articling at the City law firm, Allen & Overy.

Dr. Thomas Kell

Hamburg

Thomas Kell lead the management appraisal practice of Heidrick & Struggles in Europe and in Asia. He was with Heidrick & Struggles from 1996 until 2012, becoming a senior partner in 2002.

Prior to joining Heidrick & Struggles, Thomas was with The Boston Consulting Group. He studied mathematics and physics at the Max Planck Institute for Physics and received his diploma and doctorate from the University of Munich.

He summarized his learnings about management in his book Die Kunst der Fürhung, or The Art of Leadership, and the Harvard Business Review published an article he wrote on the primacy of corporate culture.

Adolfo Medina Haro

Mexico

Adolfo is the managing director of Medina Haro & Denogan in Mexico.

He spent nine years in search at Egon Zehnder International and four years at Heidrick & Struggles before launching his own firm. In one of its first engagements, his firm helped Vicente Fox assemble his cabinet as the newly elected president of Mexico.

Adolfo has a bachelor's degree from Universidad Anáhuac and a master's degree from the Kellogg School of Business at Northwestern. Before going into search, he worked for McKinsey and J. P. Morgan. He serves on the board of directors of Galerias Louis C. Morton.
